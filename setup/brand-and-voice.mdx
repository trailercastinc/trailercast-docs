## Brand settings

<Note>
  Find it at: **Settings → Brand**
</Note>

Brand Settings is where your trailers stop looking like AI output and start looking like your sales material. Set this up once during onboarding and every trailer your team produces from that point forward inherits the same identity, intro slide, transitions, end card, narration tone, music. Skipping this step is the difference between a polished asset prospects share internally and another generic AI doohickey.

### Brand identity essentials

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Logo" icon="image">
    PNG, ideally 1024×1024 or larger, transparent background. Used on the share-page header, trailer end cards, and as a watermark on free-tier trailers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Primary color" icon="paintbrush">
    Hex code (e.g. `#0891b2`). The accent color on share pages and Buyer Spaces.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Secondary color" icon="droplet">
    Hex code. Used for text overlays, transition tints, and lower-third backgrounds so trailers feel multi-toned instead of monochromatic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Company name" icon="building">
    How it appears on share pages and in narration when the AI references "your team."
  </Card>
  <Card title="Brand font" icon="font">
    Optional Google Font name (e.g. "Inter", "Montserrat"). Applied to text overlays, agenda slide, lower-thirds, and end cards. Falls back to a clean sans-serif if not specified.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Trailer composition assets

These get baked into every trailer the moment you upload them. The AI reuses them automatically, no per-trailer setup.

| Asset | What it is | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|
| **Intro slide** | Optional opening frame (3–5 seconds) | First frame of every trailer |
| **Outro / end card** | Optional closing frame with logo + CTA | Last frame of every trailer |
| **Agenda slide** | Auto-populated with the chapter list for THIS trailer | After the intro slide, sets expectations |
| **Transition slides** | Brief 0.5–1s slides between chapters | Between every clip in the trailer |
| **Lower-third graphic** | Overlay with chapter title and speaker name | Bottom of frame during clips |
| **Background music** | Optional soft track underlying narration | Throughout the trailer |
| **Trailer template** | Locks the order: intro → agenda → clips → outro | Applied to every composed trailer |

<Tip>
  **Why this matters**: prospects can't tell the trailer was AI-generated. It looks like your team spent a day in After Effects on each one. The AI does the picking and the composition; the brand assets are the difference between "obviously made by software" and "made by your team, polished by software."
</Tip>

### Updating brand assets later

Re-uploads only apply to **future** trailers. To backfill an updated brand into existing trailers, use **Rebuild with different clips** on each demo, the rebuild step uses your current brand assets, not the ones at original-build time.

## Voice cloning

<Note>
  Find it at: **Settings → Voice**. Available on Pro tier and above.
</Note>

If you'd like trailer narration in your own voice (or your CEO's, with their permission), record a 10–30 second voice sample and upload it. The AI uses it as the narration voice on every trailer your account produces.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record a sample">
    Read 10–30 seconds of any text in a quiet room. The AI can clone from a short sample but more is better, aim for 20s minimum.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload">
    MP3 or WAV. Click **Upload voice sample**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait ~30 seconds">
    The voice gets registered with our voice provider. You'll see "Voice cloned ✓" when it's ready.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test it">
    Process a demo. The trailer's narration uses your cloned voice automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Don't want voice narration at all? Set narration to **off** in your trailer preferences. Trailers ship with original audio only.
</Tip>

## Catalogues

Pro+ tiers also include:

- **Language catalogue**, narration in 20+ languages

Both available in **Settings → Voice**.
